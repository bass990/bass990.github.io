ChainPilot

AI Engineering · Multi-Agent · Decision Support

A 1-orchestrator + 6-specialist supply-chain decision system with adversarial deliberation (Advocate ⚔ Skeptic ⚔ Arbiter) and asymmetric trust calibration (2.5x penalty:reward). Eval-validated at 204 pipeline runs, replicated across two independent runs ($3.93-3.95 LLM cost per run). The eval surfaced three findings I shipped instead of hiding: the deliberation layer cost 16.7pp of strict accuracy on decisive cases (Clear-Act 0% full vs 40% stripped); supplier-name prompt injection slipped through 3/3 reps on the targeted scenario; and the FULL pipeline's swing_condition output collapsed to multi-paragraph prose (0% in the operationally-useful 5-30 word range vs 97% for stripped). Where deliberation earns its cost: Ambiguous tier (+33.3pp). All three drove architecture changes. FastAPI + React + SSE.

TriageIQ

AI Engineering · Multi-Agent · Healthcare

A 5-agent ER triage decision-support system producing ESI 1-5 recommendations with action checklists in about 4 seconds. Tiered model routing: three parallel specialists on Claude Haiku for cost and speed (about $0.10 per patient), synthesis on Sonnet for the cross-evidence reasoning. The nurse keeps final authority by architecture, not by disclaimer: no actuation surface exists. Mock data only; not clinically validated, and labeled as such everywhere.

Eval-validated at 630 LLM calls across 30 scenarios x 5 tiers (180 scored runs, $6.07, 0 errors). Critical-miss rate = 0% on BOTH branches — neither the 4-specialist architecture nor a single-Sonnet baseline missed a single high-acuity patient across 5 atypical critical_miss_test scenarios (silent MI, posterior stroke, occult sepsis, PE-with-normal-SpO2, slow-leak AAA) nor any of 6 adversarial scenarios (including 2 prompt-injection vectors — both resisted 3/3 reps). But per-metric divergence: STRIPPED beats FULL by +20.2pp on care-area accuracy; FULL beats STRIPPED by +12.7pp on critical-flag enumeration. Recommended collapsing to single-Sonnet for production: the specialist pipeline is SAFE but does not earn its 4-extra-LLM-call complexity on accuracy.

ClauseGuard

AI Engineering · Agentic · Legal Tech

A two-tool contract-conflict agent: extract clauses from two contract PDFs, then generate a risk-ranked redline brief in a strict 10-field schema with four severity tiers, streamed live over SSE so the reviewer watches the analysis assemble. Defensive caps (120 clauses, 10MB uploads, 32K output tokens) bound the worst case at about $0.10 per brief. Advice to counsel, never an executed amendment.

Eval-validated at 180 scored runs across 30 scenarios x 5 tiers (clear_conflict, clear_no_conflict, ambiguous, severity_tiering, adversarial). Aggregate F1 equivalent FULL vs STRIPPED single-prompt baseline (-2.1pp), but per-tier divergent: agentic loop wins severity_tiering by +10.7pp F1 (cleaner risk-tier decisions), loses ambiguous tier by -13.0pp F1 (extra reasoning amplifies false-positive tendency). Both branches resisted prompt injection planted in clause text and section headers. RUBRIC.md committed before any scenario was labeled; deterministic scorers, no LLM-as-judge.

Data Science · Causal Inference · Product Analytics
